Add concepts for new lessons. Use existing categories: 'institutions', 'financial-system', 'money'.

Lesson 2-6 (Bank Types):
- community-bank: Small local bank serving households and small businesses
- regional-bank: Bank operating across a large region or several states
- money-center-bank: Large bank relying on wholesale funding in global markets
- retail-banking: Banking focused on households through local branches
- wholesale-banking: Banking focused on large institutional clients

Lesson 2-7 (Bank Funding):
- transaction-account: Deposit allowing immediate withdrawals (checking)
- certificate-of-deposit: Time deposit with penalty for early withdrawal
- federal-funds-market: Market for overnight interbank reserve lending
- repurchase-agreement: Short-term loan secured by securities (repo)
- bank-capital: Equity funding from shareholders

Lesson 2-8 (Bank Lending):
- collateral: Assets pledged to secure a loan
- covenants: Contractual clauses restricting borrower behavior
- mortgage: Loan secured by residential real estate
- consumer-credit: Loans to households and individuals
- commercial-lending: Loans to businesses for operations and investment
- relationship-lending: Lending based on long-term private information

Lesson 2-9 (Lending Process):
- origination: Evaluating borrower and granting loan
- servicing: Managing loan after issuance (payments, monitoring)
- originate-and-hold: Traditional model keeping loans on balance sheet
- originate-and-distribute: Modern model selling loans to investors
- securitization: Process of pooling loans into tradable securities

Lesson 2-10 (Investment Banking):
- securities-underwriting: Creating and selling new securities to investors
- ipo: Initial public offering - first public stock sale
- book-building: Process of gauging demand and setting issue price
- underwriting-spread: Difference between issuer price and investor price
- syndicated-loan: Large loan funded by group of lenders
- market-making: Providing liquidity by quoting buy/sell prices
- proprietary-trading: Trading using bank's own capital

Lesson 2-11 (Balance Sheet):
- banking-book: Assets and liabilities for traditional lending
- trading-book: Assets held for short-term trading
- mark-to-market: Valuing assets at current market prices

Lesson 2-12 (Off-Balance Sheet):
- loan-commitment: Binding promise to provide funds up to limit
- contingent-liability: Potential obligation that may become real
- notional-amount: Face value for calculating derivative payments
- credit-default-swap: Insurance-like derivative against default

Lesson 2-13 (Income Statement):
- net-interest-income: Interest income minus interest expense
- net-interest-margin: NII divided by interest-earning assets
- non-interest-income: Revenue from fees and services
- loan-loss-provision: Expense for expected loan defaults

Use relatedConcepts to link related terms. Follow existing pattern with lessonId.
